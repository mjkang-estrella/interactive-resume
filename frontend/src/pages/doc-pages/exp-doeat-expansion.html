Regional Expansion

Background

Doeat was a Series-A food-delivery startup with about 50 employees, focused on serving single-person households through a hyperlocal delivery model. At the time, the service was operating in only one city. Following a successful fundraising round, the company aimed to expand rapidly to new regions.

As the company had no experienced Growth PM, I was brought in temporarily to lead regional expansion and design the go-to-market (GTM) strategy by coordinating across sales, marketing, customer support, and product teams.

Problem

The challenge was how to expand from a single-city operation to multiple cities while maintaining quality and efficiency.

Because of the hyperlocal model, Doeat’s success depended on:

  • Reliable local restaurant partnerships,
  • Understanding region-specific customer preferences, and
  • Avoiding logistical overload that could break the brand promise of “fast, freshly-cooked delivery.”

There was also no established playbook for expansion — each region had unique demand patterns and partner expectations.

Solution

  1. Market & Behavioral Analysis
    • Conducted data analysis and local interviews to identify the most attractive product categories for early adoption.
    • Found that coffee delivery was the most appealing entry point — low cost, high frequency, and flexible timing compared to meals.
  2. Local Partnership Strategy
    • Built pre-launch relationships with local cafés and small restaurants in target districts.
    • Structured deals that guaranteed predictable order volumes in exchange for exclusive promotions.
  3. Event-Based Growth Campaigns
    • Designed a “Free Coffee Weekend” campaign in each new city, offering limited-time free or discounted coffee deliveries.
    • Users could only participate by joining the Doeat Club membership, creating a strong conversion trigger.
    • Coordinated daily delivery caps to keep operations within the “30-minute delivery promise.”
  4. Cross-Team Orchestration
    • Collaborated with sales, marketing, and customer service to run synchronized regional launches.
    • Introduced structured post-launch reviews to optimize campaigns for subsequent cities.

Result & Analytics

The campaign achieved 50% membership penetration within the first month of launch in new cities, with over 3,000 pre-registrations and 800+ coffee orders sold out in just two days during initial events.

Within weeks, Doeat successfully expanded from one to five cities, proving the scalability of the model and positioning the company for future national rollout.
