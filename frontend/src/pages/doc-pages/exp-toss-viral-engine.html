Scalable Viral Loop

Background

After successfully achieving record-breaking acquisition results through reward and referral programs, our next challenge at Toss Bank was to design a sustainable viral-growth system — one that could continue to generate user inflow organically without relying on costly incentives or repetitive campaigns.

Traditional referral models reward users only when they invite new users, which creates a natural ceiling: once a user’s friends have already joined, the loop stops. For a bank that had already surpassed six million customers, this model was becoming inefficient and unsustainable.

Problem

The core issue was how to build a viral loop that doesn’t die after the first wave of adoption.

Existing referral systems:

  • Required users to find unregistered friends manually — increasing friction.
  • Offered one-time incentives that quickly lost novelty.

We needed a mechanism that allowed continuous sharing among existing users while still appealing to new users through a contextual, emotionally resonant experience.

Solution

  1. Behavioral Psychology Framework
    • Designed the system around intrinsic motivators: social reciprocity, emotional connection, and seasonal relevance.
    • Shifted the growth model from transactional referrals to social gestures — messages users wanted to send, not had to.
  2. Redesigned Viral Loop Architecture
    • Created a structure where members could share with both members and non-members, earning small, repeatable rewards (under one cent per share).
    • This ensured the loop remained self-sustaining, as users could participate repeatedly without exhausting their network.
  3. Seasonal Event Campaigns
    • Developed 10+ viral campaigns within six months, each themed around emotional, culturally relevant moments such as Korean Thanksgiving (Chuseok) and New Year’s greetings.
    • Each campaign provided a message template users could personalize and share — optimized for speed and social spread.
  4. Scalable System & Modularization
    • Built the entire viral engine as a reusable module, documented its architecture, and shared it across squads.
    • Enabled teams managing other products (e.g., foreign currency accounts, savings products) to easily plug in the viral system.

Result & Analytics

The Korean Thanksgiving campaign alone generated 100M+ shares within 72 hours, attracting 200K new users in just three days.

After being scaled across multiple squads, the system drove 3M+ additional new accounts, representing ~40% of Toss Bank’s total customer base. Beyond growth, the viral engine became a core internal framework for how Toss Bank designed behaviorally-driven growth mechanics — turning user emotion and social connection into a scalable acquisition system.
