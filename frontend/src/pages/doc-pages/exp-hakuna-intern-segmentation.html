Funnel Optimization

Background

When I joined Hyperconnect's CPO team as a product strategy intern, my mission was to analyze the company's flagship apps, Azar and Hakuna Live, through the lens of an external user. The goal was to surface overlooked pain points and growth opportunities without the bias of internal familiarity. I was given broad strategic context and freedom to explore, which led me to spend weeks using both apps, observing user flows, and documenting behavioral patterns.

Problem

Despite strong user acquisition, the engagement and conversion rates between usage stages were inconsistent. There was no clear understanding of what actions differentiated Beginners, Enthusiasts, and Maniacs—the three main engagement levels I identified. Without this behavioral clarity, the team made product decisions around onboarding, retention loops, and monetization without a strong analytical foundation.

Solution

  1. User Segmentation Framework: I classified users into three engagement tiers — Beginners, Enthusiasts, and Maniacs — based on frequency, interaction depth, and social behaviors.
  2. Funnel Mapping & Hypothesis Development: For each tier, I identified the actions required to move users to the next stage and mapped the drop-off points preventing that progression.
  3. Quantitative Validation via Amplitude: After the CPO approved my qualitative findings, I gained access to internal Amplitude data covering over 3 million user events. I used it to:
    • Measure the conversion rate between engagement tiers.
    • Identify specific bottlenecks in the interaction funnel.
    • Compare as-is vs to-be funnel improvements based on behavioral data.
  4. Prioritization & Strategic Proposal: Using the insights, I proposed targeted feature improvements — such as simplified entry points and incentive triggers — ranked by potential impact on engagement and conversion.

Result & Analytics

  • The refined segmentation and funnel analysis directly informed feature prioritization for 2021, driving a 15% increase in core user conversion rate.
  • The work was personally endorsed by the CEO, leading to broader adoption of user segmentation frameworks across other product lines.
  • This experience marked my first end-to-end cycle of connecting qualitative UX observation with quantitative behavioral data, reinforcing my approach to product decisions as data-informed and user-driven.
