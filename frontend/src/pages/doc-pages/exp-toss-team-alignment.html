Team Satisfaction

Background

As a Product Manager in a cross-functional Squad of 6â€“8 members including designers, banking operations, frontend/backend engineers, and data analysts, my goal was to improve both execution speed and team stability. Each squad worked autonomously, so clear communication and shared context were critical to alignment.

Problem

The challenge was maintaining consistent alignment and motivation across disciplines as projects scaled. Without structured feedback loops or shared OKRs, teams risked communication gaps, duplicated efforts, and uneven visibility into strategic priorities.

Solution

  1. Structured Feedback System:
    • Initiated 1:1 coffee chats every four weeks for mutual feedback and individual health checks.
    • Encouraged open discussions around workload balance, product direction, and inter-team dependencies.
  2. Quarterly OKR Framework:
    • Introduced a quarterly OKR-setting process that aligned squad objectives with company-wide priorities.
    • Ensured every team member understood how their goals contributed to broader outcomes.
  3. Weekly Context Syncs:
    • Hosted weekly alignment sessions to share executive-level updates and cross-squad learnings, helping the team stay context-aware and empowered to propose new ideas autonomously.
  4. Culture of Transparency and Empowerment:
    • Created a culture where designers, engineers, and analysts could initiate discussions on roadmap priorities, improving ownership and engagement.

Result & Analytics

The initiative achieved 95% team satisfaction, 20% faster feature delivery, and significantly improved overall team retention. More importantly, it demonstrated how intentional leadership and structured communication could accelerate product development while sustaining a healthy, motivated team culture.
