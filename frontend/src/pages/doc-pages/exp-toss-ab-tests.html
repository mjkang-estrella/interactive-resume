Funnel Optimization

Background

To optimize Toss Bank’s account-opening funnel, I led an initiative to continuously identify and remove friction points across the user acquisition journey. The funnel served as a critical growth lever, directly affecting conversion rates and overall marketing efficiency.

Problem

While top-of-funnel traffic was growing rapidly, conversion had room of improvement between landing and account completion. Heatmaps, event logs, and user interviews revealed usability gaps — unclear value messaging, long forms, and confusing microcopy. The challenge was not just identifying these issues, but testing and iterating fast enough to validate improvements in a live environment.

Solution

  1. Rapid Experimentation Framework:
    • Built an internal process for running A/B tests within 1–3 days from idea to release.
    • Partnered closely with designers and developers to shorten review cycles and automate experiment deployment.
  2. Data + Qualitative Insights:
    • Combined quantitative funnel analysis (drop-off points, completion time) with qualitative user feedback to prioritize high-impact hypotheses.
  3. Iterative Funnel Optimization:
    • Conducted over 15 rapid A/B tests on onboarding copy, page hierarchy, input-field order, and loading transitions.
    • Used real-time dashboards to monitor conversion deltas and end conditions for each test.
  4. Cross-Functional Communication:
    • Shared learnings weekly to standardize best-performing UX patterns across acquisition and marketing teams.

Result & Analytics

The rapid iteration cycle increased funnel conversion rate by 15% within weeks and became a model for agile product experimentation inside Toss Bank. By proving that data-driven design and fast execution could coexist, the initiative set a new operational standard for product velocity and evidence-based decision-making.
