Challenge Account

Background

The Social Squad at Toss Bank aimed to strengthen user lock-in through social and behavioral features, following the company’s rapid growth in activation metrics driven by the Growth Squad. We wanted to design a product that not only attracted users but also encouraged long-term engagement within the bank ecosystem.

Inspired by the insight that people struggle with waking up early, we decided to link this behavior with a financial incentive — creating a group challenge product called the Challenge Account, where participants could deposit money and receive rewards based on successful participation.

Problem

The initial idea involved redistributing deposits to users who successfully completed the morning challenge, but it faced regulatory restrictions: banks are not allowed to create products where users can lose their principal. This required a fundamental redesign of the business logic while keeping the original motivation intact.

Solution

  1. Legal & Structural Pivot:

    Collaborated with the Legal and Compliance teams to rebuild the product around interest-based rewards instead of pooled deposits. The system transformed into a lottery-style structure, where accumulated interest funded prizes for winners — fully compliant yet still motivating.

  2. Engagement Optimization:

    After rapid initial growth, we noticed a stagnation in participation. We iterated the model by introducing multiple prize tiers (1st–5th place) to ensure that more users experienced wins, which reignited engagement and growth.

  3. Community Features:

    Added social mechanics such as a morning chat room accessible only to early participants, reinforcing the product’s community feel and emotional stickiness.

Result & Analytics

The Challenge Account became the fastest-growing product in company history, achieving 300K+ MAU within one month, 70%+ week-1 retention, and ultimately reducing company-wide churn by 5%. Beyond the numbers, the project demonstrated how navigating constraints like financial regulations could spark innovative, compliant solutions that still deliver user delight.
