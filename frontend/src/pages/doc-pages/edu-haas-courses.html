MBA Courseworks

Why MBA?

I came to Haas to bridge three threads of my career — product management, data, and purpose.

Before the MBA, I’d built consumer products at Toss Bank and Hyperconnect, where I learned how to ship at scale and translate data into user impact. But I realized that to grow into a global product leader, I needed to strengthen two areas: strategic breadth and human depth by understanding how technology, business models, and human behavior come together.

Haas was a natural choice because its culture of “Beyond Yourself” and focus on responsible innovation align with how I want to build products — ones that are useful, ethical, and globally scalable. Here, I’m exploring how AI and data governance shape next-generation fintech and health products, while also improving my leadership through cross-functional teamwork and storytelling.

Agentic AI (CS Department)

As a product manager, understanding AI's capabilities and business impact is crucial, but knowing how to implement it is equally important for building and deploying real-world solutions. That's why I took the Agentic AI course, even though it's from the CS department and focuses on technical implementation.

Agentic AI is an emerging field that promises to transform daily life through intelligent task automation and personalization. This course covered:

  1. Fundamental concepts essential for Agentic AI (LLM foundations, reasoning, planning, agentic frameworks, infrastructure)
  2. Representative agent applications (e.g. code generation, robotics, web automation, scientific discovery)
  3. Examine the limitations and potential risks of current LLM agents and share insights into directions for further improvement.

The Ethics of AI (Philosophy Department)

I've always been interested in the philosophical implications of AI. As AI rapidly improves, it raises ethical questions around safety, usage, and personality. I've already written several blog posts about this (AI and Philosophy, ChatGPT-Companionship). I took this course to extend this interest further and discuss ethical questions with students from other departments.

This course covered multiple topics related to AI ethics, including inherent bias, privacy and surveillance, opacity, hallucinations, manipulation, and impact on education.

Entrepreneurship

Product management is about identifying user problems and building solutions. I have extensive experience scaling products from 1 to 10 and 10 to 100, but I haven't built anything from 0 to 1, except for individual features within established applications. That's why I took this entrepreneurship class: to learn the fundamentals and gain hands-on experience in early-stage product development.
