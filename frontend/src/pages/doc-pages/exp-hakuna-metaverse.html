Metaverse Live

Background

Due to the intense competition within the live streaming market and the stagnation of existing user engagement, expanding both target markets and user segments became a strategic necessity.

To achieve this, the team sought to introduce Metaverse experiences into the Live Streaming ecosystem, aligning with emerging market trends to attract new users and drive deeper engagement among existing ones.

Over the course of a year, we designed and executed a multi-phase roadmap, defining key problems and corresponding solutions for each iteration. Throughout this process, multiple directional pivots were made based on user feedback and behavioral data.

Iteration 1: Live Festa – Discover

Problem

  • New users struggled to navigate and discover live rooms within the platform.
  • The onboarding funnel from room discovery → interaction → follow showed significant drop-offs, indicating friction in exploration and engagement.

Solution

We introduced Live Festa, a map-based discovery feature that allows users to explore various live rooms spatially—creating a more intuitive and gamified entry point to the platform.

Result

  • In A/B testing, new users who explored through the Live Festa map demonstrated over 20% longer session duration compared to those who used traditional browsing.
  • However, the introduction of preview playback led to a drop in live room entry rate, and overall D1 retention remained largely unchanged.

Iteration 2: Virtual Live Room

Problem

  • Despite positive feedback, Live Festa did not produce meaningful retention gains.
  • Given the nature of live streaming services, in-room experiences proved to have a greater impact on engagement than discovery features.
  • Data showed that users spent over 80% of their session time within live rooms, suggesting that improvements should focus on enhancing that core environment.

Solution

We developed Virtual Live Room, allowing users to control avatars and freely move within a virtual map inside the live stream.

This aimed to increase immersion and encourage longer session durations by creating a sense of co-presence and interaction.

Result

The feature achieved an initial usage rate exceeding 40%, and average time spent increased by 5% compared to regular rooms. However, these improvements were not sustained over time.

User interviews and surveys indicated two primary issues:

  • Inability to switch between room types limited exploration.
  • Lack of engaging content within the map reduced replay value.

Iteration 3: Virtual Live Room – Minigame

Problem

  • The Virtual Live Room successfully drew initial interest but lacked long-term engagement drivers.
  • The experience relied heavily on streamers’ individual creativity, resulting in inconsistent quality and low repeat usage.
  • Moreover, the feature did not effectively attract new users at scale.

Solution

To strengthen in-room engagement, we added interactive minigame content—starting with an O/X (True or False) quiz format.

The platform provided a base game framework while allowing streamers to customize the experience and leverage their personalities, striking a balance between platform guidance and creator freedom.

Result

  • The feature achieved over 100,000 monthly active users (MAU) at its peak.
  • Average session time doubled compared to Iteration 2.
  • Click-through rate (CTR) improved by 3.6x, and cost per acquisition (CPA) decreased by 19%.
  • However, usage gradually declined again due to content maintenance and operational overhead, highlighting the need for sustainable content pipelines.
