Reactivation Program

Background

As Doeat’s user base grew, we noticed through churn analysis that a large portion of inactive users were women—a segment under-served by the platform’s mostly male-oriented meal offerings. To re-engage these users, I designed and launched a reactivation campaign built around personalized incentives and emotional relevance rather than generic discounts.

Problem

The key challenge was how to win back churned users whose needs the existing product didn’t address. Purely digital CRM messaging had limited impact, and our internal data suggested that these users disengaged because they didn’t feel personally valued or represented in Doeat’s product lineup.

Solution

  1. Data & User Insight:
    • Identified female-dominant churn segments through behavioral and demographic data.
    • Validated insights via short follow-up interviews and survey responses highlighting low emotional connection with the brand.
  2. Personalized Reactivation Concept:
    • Partnered with popular local dessert cafés—a product category preferred by our target segment.
    • Personally sourced and delivered premium desserts over weekends to ensure quality and authenticity.
  3. Integrated CRM Campaign:
    • Sent multi-channel messages (SMS, push, and chat) to churned users offering a free dessert in exchange for membership re-enrollment.
    • Designed the flow so users could easily reactivate accounts with a single tap and join membership during checkout.
  4. Execution Leadership:
    • Coordinated cross-functionally with marketing, logistics, and customer support under a tight one-week timeline.
    • Managed all on-site logistics myself—from café pickups to last-mile coordination—to ensure flawless user experience.

Result & Analytics

The campaign tripled dormant-user returns, increased membership conversions fivefold, and maintained twice the long-term retention compared to the baseline cohort. Beyond metrics, the project became a cultural example of hands-on, execution-first ability, demonstrating that meaningful growth sometimes requires literally going the extra mile for users.
