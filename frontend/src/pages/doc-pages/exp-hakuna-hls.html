Live Streaming Infrastructure

Background

  • As a live streaming service, improving overall broadcast quality and reliability was a top priority.
  • We hypothesized that higher-quality streaming would directly lead to increased viewer satisfaction, longer watch times, and higher overall engagement.

Iteration 1: Video & Audio Quality Improvement

Problem

  • Hakuna’s live streams were originally limited to 480p resolution, which led to frequent user complaints about low visual quality.
  • This negatively impacted both perceived platform quality and user retention, especially in regions with better network infrastructure.

Solution

  • Upgraded the maximum streaming resolution to 720p, while introducing adaptive bitrate streaming to dynamically switch between 480p and 720p based on device performance and network conditions.
  • After evaluating trade-offs between infrastructure cost, user satisfaction, and global network environments, we selected 720p instead of 1080p as the optimal resolution.

Result

  • Received positive feedback from streamers and viewers regarding improved broadcast quality.
  • The number of customer service complaints related to low resolution dropped by over 60%.

Iteration 2: HLS-Based PC Broadcasting Infrastructure

Background

  • Hakuna’s existing live streaming protocol was WebRTC, optimized for low-latency mobile streaming.
  • However, professional streamers typically use OBS (Open Broadcaster Software) for screen composition and output, which only supports Virtual Camera and HLS streaming.

Problem

  • While video quality had improved, demand for professional PC streaming increased rapidly.
  • To support both casual mobile users and professional streamers, Hakuna aimed to expand its ecosystem by providing a studio-grade broadcasting environment.
  • The existing WebRTC architecture, however, imposed limitations on advanced content creation and professional production.

Solution

  • Built a new HLS-based streaming infrastructure to enable PC broadcasting via OBS, supporting 1080p resolution and 60fps frame rate.
  • This allowed professional streamers to broadcast with higher flexibility, better visuals, and multi-screen composition capabilities.

Result

  • HLS streamers showed 25% more viewers per broadcast and 9% longer average session duration.
  • Collected highly positive feedback from professional streamers.
  • However, compared to WebRTC, HLS incurred higher latency (1s → 7s delay) and significantly higher infrastructure costs (~$300/user/month).
  • Therefore, the feature was limited to curated events and professional streamers, rather than full-scale rollout.

Iteration 3: WebRTC-Based PC Broadcasting Infrastructure

Problem

  • While HLS confirmed the demand and impact of PC broadcasting, its latency and cost made it unsuitable for full deployment.
  • A more scalable and cost-efficient infrastructure was needed for general user adoption.

Solution

  • Developed a WebRTC-based PC broadcasting system, leveraging the existing low-latency protocol.
  • Although limited to 720p resolution and 30fps, it offered better accessibility, minimal delay, and lower operational cost.
  • This provided a more inclusive option for everyday streamers while maintaining acceptable quality.

Result

  • The adoption rate of PC streaming increased over 4x after the rollout.
  • Viewers of WebRTC PC broadcasts exhibited over 10% higher average watch time than traditional mobile broadcasts.
  • This established a sustainable and scalable foundation for hybrid mobile–PC streaming growth.
