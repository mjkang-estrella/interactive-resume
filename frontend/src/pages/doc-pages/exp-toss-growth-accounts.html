Growth Hacking

Background

When I joined Toss Bank, the company had just launched but lacked a dedicated Growth team. I became one of the four founding members of the new Growth Squad — a small, cross-functional unit composed of one PM (myself), one designer, and two engineers.

Our mission was clear yet daunting:

  • Achieve the fastest user growth in Korean banking history,
  • Maintain CAC under $3.5 / user, and
  • Sustain 80%+ week-one retention while driving profitability through strong LTV/CAC efficiency.

Unlike traditional banks relying on large marketing budgets, we needed to build organic, scalable, and data-driven growth systems that could sustain themselves even with minimal paid acquisition.

Problem

The core challenge was how to drive exponential acquisition without over-spending or compromising retention.

We faced three constraints:

  1. Limited marketing resources compared to incumbents.
  2. Brand new product positioning — no user trust or brand equity yet.
  3. A need for repeatable, viral growth engines that could sustain without ongoing heavy incentives.

We needed to find a way to make users themselves the distribution channel — turning product delight into a natural acquisition engine.

Solution

  1. Launched Reward-Driven Activation Loop
    • Designed an onboarding reward program that offered small cash bonuses for new account openings.
    • This initial campaign created the first acquisition surge, helping seed a viral user base.
  2. Built Organic Viral Mechanisms
    • Developed a share-and-invite system inside the banking app — users could share their personalized account link or challenge activity.
    • Added social triggers (e.g., “Your friend just opened a Toss Bank account”) to drive FOMO-based virality.
    • Introduced Twitter-based sharing and limited-time campaigns, leading to mass social diffusion.
  3. Collaborated for Scalable Marketing & SEO
    • Worked with the marketing team to optimize app store listings and SEO for major keywords and improve organic discovery.
    • Partnered with influencers and content creators to promote new savings and challenge products, leveraging social proof as a growth accelerant.
  4. Data-Driven Experimentation
    • Built dashboards to monitor cohort-level retention, CAC, and viral coefficient (K-factor).
    • Conducted A/B tests on copy, incentive size, and sharing UX.
    • Prioritized experiments using ICE scoring to maximize ROI from a small team.
  5. Iterative Viral System Development (more on next bullet)
    • Over time, we designed a full viral loop system — allowing users to challenge, share, and invite others seamlessly inside the product.
    • This not only fueled acquisition but also strengthened early engagement, as new users joined through trusted social connections.

Result & Analytics

The Growth Squad’s initiatives led to 2.1 million new account openings within six months, achieving +30% user growth and establishing Toss Bank as Korea’s fastest-growing bank.

  • CAC: $3.5/user (record low in Korean banking).
  • Retention: Over 80% week-one sustained across cohorts.
  • Shift in focus: The scale of success allowed the company to de-prioritize acquisition and reallocate resources toward retention and engagement, leading to the creation of the Social Squad that I later joined.

This experience taught me that growth is not a single campaign but a system — the intersection of product mechanics, psychology, and data. Building that system from zero with a four-person team remains one of my proudest achievements.
