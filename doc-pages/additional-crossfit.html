CrossFit Analyzer

Tech Stack

Figma Make (Prototype) · Supabase (Backend & Auth) · OpenAI API (AI Analysis Engine)

1. Background

CrossFit workouts, known as WODs (Workout of the Day), combine constantly varied functional movements across ten domains: strength, stamina, endurance, flexibility, power, speed, coordination, agility, balance, and accuracy. Most athletes only record their scores (time, rounds, or weight) and miss insight into which domains they actually improve. Over time it becomes difficult to identify weaknesses, track balanced progress, or program future training cycles.

2. Problem

Athletes log WODs inconsistently across notebooks, spreadsheets, and apps, with no standardized way to analyze domain balance (e.g., power vs. endurance focus). Existing tools concentrate on score tracking rather than performance insight, leaving both coaches and athletes without a clear picture of how training distributions shape their fitness profile.

3. Solution

CrossFit Analyzer automatically parses every WOD, classifies movements, and generates a domain-based performance profile.

  • WOD Recorder: streamlined logging interface for results and movements.
  • Movement Library: rich descriptions, scaling tips, and related WOD suggestions for each movement.
  • Domain Analytics: OpenAI-powered classification assigns each WOD to CrossFit’s ten domains.
  • Performance Visualization: trend plots expose domain distribution and highlight overtraining or gaps.
  • Supabase Auth + Database: secure storage for athletes and their training history.

4. Analytics & Iteration

  • Crafted an orange-accented mobile prototype with Figma Make for rapid testing.
  • Modeled Supabase tables for Users, Movements, and WOD Records to streamline ingest and querying.
  • Integrated GPT-based analysis that outputs domain splits (e.g., “70% endurance · 30% power”) per WOD.
  • Ran mock WOD inputs, refined domain mapping, sped up input UI, and simplified analytics visuals for clarity.

5. Results & Learnings

  • Demonstrated AI-driven domain analysis that boosts athlete self-awareness.
  • Delivered an end-to-end prototype: Auth → WOD Input → AI Analysis → Domain Visualization.
  • Early testers highlighted how domain-based visuals motivate balanced training over raw score chasing.

6. Next Steps

  • Integrate wearable metrics (heart rate, power output) for deeper physiological context.
  • Add domain-filterable community leaderboards to foster targeted competition.
  • Ship a coaching workspace so trainers can monitor clients’ domain balance and prescribe programming.
  • Experiment with AI-generated personalized WOD recommendations and launch an MVP to validate retention.
