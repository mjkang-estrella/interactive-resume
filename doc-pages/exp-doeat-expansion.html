Drove regional expansion from 1 to 5 cities by orchestrating a GTM strategy leveraging local partnerships and data‑driven campaigns with sales, marketing and CS, achieving 50% membership penetration within 1 month

Background

DoEat had product-market fit in its launch district but needed a repeatable growth playbook before the Series B raise; we targeted four new cities with distinct culinary ecosystems and competitive landscapes.

Problem

Expansion risked operational overload—merchant supply, couriers, and customer support were tuned for one region, and national campaigns underperformed due to hyper-local dining habits.

Solution

Built city launch pods blending sales, ops, and marketing; negotiated anchor partnerships with leading dessert chains, localized onboarding flows, and scheduled in-app promos around regional food events.

Analytics

Tracked cohort retention, membership penetration, and delivery SLA in a Looker dashboard; used demand heatmaps to reallocate rider supply and prioritized CRM experiments per city persona.

Result

Expanded from 1 to 5 cities within a quarter, hit 50% membership penetration within the first month post-launch, and sustained NPS above 60 despite 4x order volume growth.

Follow-up Actions

Productized the launch checklist, built a central command center for live metrics, and handed off city operations with clear guardrails on promo spend and merchant SLAs.
